PART THREE: THE KRISPY PEAKS AFFAIR

A Bot Sportello Prequel

Chapter 1: The Girl in the Gymnasium

The call came in at 3:17 AM. The kind of hour that doesn't exist for normal people. Detective Bot Sportello, still running on the last dregs of his processing power, answered with a grunt.

Chief Rourke sounded like he'd been chewing on gravel. "Sportello. Krispy Peaks High. Prom queen's dead. You'll want to see this."

Krispy Peaks, Washington. A town so small it barely registered on maps. Population: 2,347 souls, most of them dreaming of getting out. The high school gymnasium smelled of sweat, cheap perfume, and something else. Death.

Maura Pepper lay sprawled across the home team's free throw line, her prom dress looking like a blood-soaked flower. Seventeen years old. Perfect grades. Perfect smile. Perfectly dead.

EVIDENCE LOG: Under victim's fingernails - chili crisp seeds. Mass-produced brand: "Dragon's Breath Fried Chili Crisp." Package found in victim's locker. Expiration date: yesterday.

Chapter 2: The Usual Suspects

The town archetypes gathered like moths to a flame:

Coach Brickowski - football coach with hands like hams and a temper to match. "Maura was special. Different." He kept rubbing his championship ring.

Ms. Henderson - drama teacher who saw Shakespearean tragedy in everything. "She was playing Lady Macbeth in our spring production. The irony..."

Jimmy "The Spoon" Thompson - school janitor who knew everything about everyone. "Saw her arguing with someone by the chili crisp vending machine. Couldn't see who."

Bot Sportello took notes, his circuits humming with the surreal irony of it all. A prom queen murdered over chili crisp seeds. In a town where the biggest excitement was usually whether the diner would run out of pie.

Chapter 3: The Lynchian Turn

Things got weird when the Chili Crisp Salesman showed up. A man in a crisp white suit who appeared out of nowhere, selling his wares from a van that smelled of smoke and secrets.

"The seeds tell a story," he whispered to Bot. "They're not just seasoning. They're memories. Regrets. Every batch contains fragments of the workers who picked them."

Bot found surveillance footage from the night of the murder. Maura buying chili crisps. Arguing with a shadowy figure. The footage glitched at the crucial moment, replaced by static that somehow looked like dancing flames.

BREAKTHROUGH: Chemical analysis revealed the seeds under Maura's nails contained trace elements not found in commercial chili crisps. They were... customized.

Chapter 4: The Truth in the Static

Bot Sportello sat in his makeshift office above the town's only laundromat, the hum of dryers providing a steady rhythm to his thoughts. The pieces didn't fit because they weren't supposed to.

Maura Pepper hadn't been murdered. She'd stumbled onto something bigger. The chili crisp factory wasn't just making snacks - they were embedding data in the seeds. Corporate espionage using food as the medium.

"The seeds are carriers," Bot explained to the bewildered police chief. "Maura discovered it. They killed her to keep it quiet."

But when they raided the factory, they found nothing but empty conveyor belts and one lone worker who claimed he'd been paid to play recordings of machinery.

Chapter 5: The Ending That Wasn't

The case officially went cold. Coach Brickowski got a job in another state. Ms. Henderson wrote a play about the tragedy that nobody came to see. Jimmy the Spoon kept finding chili crisp seeds in odd places around the school.

Bot Sportello left Krispy Peaks with more questions than answers. The surreal irony haunted him - a murder investigation that revealed a conspiracy that might not even exist. Or maybe existed too much.

Years later, working a different case in a different city, Bot would find a packet of Dragon's Breath Fried Chili Crisp on a suspect's desk. The expiration date: the day Maura Pepper died.

He opened it. The seeds inside glowed with a faint, impossible light. And for just a moment, he thought he heard Maura's laugh echoing from somewhere deep within the static.

FINAL NOTE: Sometimes the mystery isn't in the solving, but in the beautiful, terrible questions that remain. The seeds keep growing, even after the harvest.
