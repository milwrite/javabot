Reveal Digital: Comprehensive Analysis of Open Access Primary Source Collections Through Collaborative Library Digitization

Reveal Digital represents an innovative approach to digital preservation and open access publishing through its unique library crowdfunding model, pooling resources from academic institutions and libraries to digitize and make freely available primary source collections that center on underrepresented voices from the twentieth century and beyond.1,3,1 As a division of JSTOR and part of the larger ITHAKA organization, Reveal Digital has successfully developed and published over one million pages of openly available content across seven major collections focused on social movements, marginalized communities, and voices of dissent.1,1 This comprehensive analysis examines the organizational structure, operational mechanisms, technical infrastructure, and transformative impact of Reveal Digital on scholarly access and historical preservation, while also addressing the complex challenges of rights clearance, metadata standards, and sustainable open access publishing models that define contemporary digital stewardship.

Understanding Reveal Digital: Mission and Organizational Purpose

Reveal Digital emerged as a direct response to significant gaps in the scholarly record, recognizing that many important historical narratives, particularly those centered on activists, artists, journalists, and communities whose experiences challenge mainstream narratives, remain largely inaccessible or underdeveloped in major research collections.1,10 The fundamental mission of Reveal Digital centers on discovering, digitizing, and preserving the lesser-known stories and voices of marginalized groups and social movements spanning the twentieth and twenty-first centuries.10,1 This mission reflects a broader commitment to advancing social justice through information access by ensuring that vital, underrepresented histories remain preserved and freely accessible to researchers, educators, and the general public worldwide.3,8,1

The organizational structure positions Reveal Digital as a collaborative initiative where libraries and cultural institutions serve not merely as passive consumers of digitized materials but as active partners in defining what gets preserved and how.1,1 This collaborative framework fundamentally transforms traditional models of collection development, where decisions about what materials warrant digitization typically rest with a handful of major research institutions. Instead, Reveal Digital redistributes this agency among a broad network of academic libraries of varying sizes and resource levels, from small liberal arts colleges to prestigious research universities.37 The result is a democratized approach to digital heritage that allows diverse institutional communities to influence the historical record being preserved for future generations.

The organizational ethos of Reveal Digital emphasizes that historical narratives should not merely reflect elite or dominant perspectives but should encompass the diverse experiences and viewpoints of communities often marginalized in traditional archives.1,3,1 By bringing together hidden voices and amplifying perspectives that challenge dominant narratives, Reveal Digital reshapes what history includes, who it represents, and crucially, who can access it.1,1 This commitment to democratizing access to primary sources extends beyond simply digitizing materials—it involves fundamentally reimagining the relationships between institutions, communities, and the historical record itself.

Organizational Context: ITHAKA, JSTOR, and Digital Stewardship

To fully understand Reveal Digital, one must situate it within the broader organizational ecosystem of ITHAKA and JSTOR, both of which serve as foundational institutions enabling Reveal Digital's operations.4,12,1 ITHAKA functions as a mission-based nonprofit organization dedicated to eliminating barriers to learning by making higher education and access to knowledge more affordable and equitable.4 Through its digital libraries and learning platforms, ITHAKA delivers uninterrupted access to collections from academic libraries, publishers, and museums around the world to millions of students, teachers, and researchers across 180 countries.4 This global reach creates infrastructure through which Reveal Digital's collections can achieve maximum visibility and accessibility to international scholarly audiences.

JSTOR, as a primary platform under the ITHAKA umbrella, serves as the primary hosting and delivery mechanism for Reveal Digital's collections, ensuring that digitized materials achieve permanent, barrier-free access with long-term preservation guarantees through Portico, a specialized digital preservation service.1,3,1 The integration with JSTOR provides Reveal Digital with robust technical infrastructure, established discovery mechanisms, and integration into existing research workflows that millions of researchers already utilize. Furthermore, ITHAKA's commitment to advancing knowledge broadly resonates deeply with Reveal Digital's own mission, creating natural alignment between organizational values and operational practices.4

The emergence of JSTOR Digital Stewardship Services represents a significant evolution in how ITHAKA supports institutional partners in managing and preserving their collections.12,30 This comprehensive platform brings together digital asset management, long-term preservation through Portico, and AI-assisted collections processing tools within a seamless, cloud-hosted environment.12 While distinct from Reveal Digital itself, JSTOR Digital Stewardship Services complements Reveal Digital's work by providing complementary tools and services for institutions seeking to process, manage, preserve, and share their own collections more broadly.12,30 The synergy between Reveal Digital's crowdfunded, collaborative approach and the broader suite of JSTOR Digital Stewardship Services demonstrates how ITHAKA's ecosystem supports multiple pathways for advancing open access to digital collections.

Reveal Digital's position within this organizational structure allows it to leverage ITHAKA's expertise, technological infrastructure, and institutional relationships while maintaining its distinctive focus on crowdfunded digitization of materials documenting marginalized voices and social movements.1,3 The nonprofit nature of ITHAKA ensures that the driving force behind these initiatives remains the advancement of knowledge and equity rather than profit maximization, a crucial distinction that shapes how decisions are made about which collections warrant inclusion and how access is provided once materials are digitized.

The Collaborative Crowdfunding Model: Innovation in Sustainable Publishing

Perhaps the most distinctive feature of Reveal Digital's operational approach is its innovative crowdfunding model, through which libraries and cultural institutions collectively pool financial resources to fund digitization and rights clearance for specific collections.1,1,37 Rather than relying on traditional grant funding or institutional budget allocations, Reveal Digital invites libraries to make pledges toward specific collection projects, with funding goals established before digitization work commences.1,3,37 This mechanism ensures that digitization only proceeds when sufficient institutional commitment exists, reducing financial risk while simultaneously creating broad stakeholder investment in collection outcomes.

The crowdfunding model operates through a transparent, collaborative process where libraries of all sizes and resource levels can participate as funders and stakeholders.37 The range of participating institutions demonstrates the accessibility of this model—the Independent Voices collection, for example, has drawn funding commitments from ninety-six libraries in the United States, ranging from four-year liberal arts colleges to the largest research universities, including Harvard, Yale, Columbia, University of Michigan, and the University of California system.37 This diversity of institutional participation ensures that collection development reflects varied perspectives and priorities rather than centering narrowly on the preferences of elite institutions.

When a project reaches its established funding goal, Reveal Digital manages the entire digitization and publication workflow, including rights clearance, metadata description, digitization quality assurance, and final hosting on JSTOR for permanent, barrier-free access.1,1,1 This comprehensive service model removes substantial technical and administrative burdens from individual libraries while ensuring consistent quality standards across all collections.1,1 Participating libraries receive digital files of the materials they contribute back for their own use, creating additional value for institutions that commit funding to projects.37 Once funded and published, collections become freely available to all users in perpetuity, creating a sustainable model for open access publishing driven by library collaboration rather than continuing subscription fees or institutional gatekeeping.1,1

A crucial innovation within this model is that libraries retain no exclusive access rights to crowdfunded collections—once published, the materials are equally available to users worldwide without subscription barriers or institutional authentication requirements.1,1,37 This represents a significant departure from traditional models where institutions might secure exclusive rights to collections they fund. By ensuring universal access, Reveal Digital maximizes the scholarly impact of digitization investments while advancing the broader mission of expanding access to knowledge.1,1

The financial sustainability of the crowdfunding model depends critically on establishing realistic funding targets and managing rights clearance efficiently. Clearing copyright permissions emerges as one of Reveal Digital's primary contributions to the digitization process, as navigating the complex landscape of copyright holders, permissions requirements, and licensing negotiations would overwhelm individual institutions attempting to manage these processes independently.13,37 By aggregating institutional resources and negotiating at scale, Reveal Digital achieves efficiencies in rights clearance that individual institutions could never accomplish alone. Additionally, when securing rights, Reveal Digital provides copyright holders with high-resolution images of their publications to use as they wish, creating reciprocal benefit and incentivizing cooperation with the rights clearance process.37

Reveal Digital Collections: Documenting Marginalized Voices and Social Movements

As of 2026, Reveal Digital has successfully developed and published seven major collections, each focusing on specific aspects of twentieth and twenty-first century social movements and underrepresented historical narratives. Understanding these collections provides concrete insight into how Reveal Digital operationalizes its mission of amplifying marginalized voices.1,7,10,1

The Independent Voices collection represents a foundational Reveal Digital undertaking, bringing together more than one thousand titles from the alternative press of the 1960s through 1980s.1,1,37 This collection encompasses underground newspapers, journals, and publications produced by diverse social movements during a transformative period in American history.37 These materials provide primary source documentation of perspectives often excluded from mainstream media archives, including voices of various counterculture movements, alternative political organizations, and grassroots journalism. The breadth and depth of this collection make it invaluable for scholars studying alternative media, social movements, and the evolution of public discourse during this turbulent historical period.

The Black Periodicals collection documents the rich literary, political, and cultural contributions of African Americans across publications spanning the Great Migration period through the Black Power era.7,22 These periodicals capture African American voices engaging in ongoing struggles for equality, justice, and self-determination during critical historical moments. By making these materials freely available, Reveal Digital ensures that researchers and students can access firsthand accounts and contemporary perspectives from Black communities themselves rather than relying exclusively on secondary accounts written from outside these communities. The collection's relevance extends beyond historical research into contemporary educational contexts, where these primary sources enrich understanding of African American history and ongoing struggles for racial justice.

The American Prison Newspapers collection captures voices from incarcerated individuals across nearly two centuries of American history, with publications ranging from the 1800s to the present day.7,21 Prison newspapers represent unique documentary evidence of conditions within carceral institutions, resistance to unjust systems, and the humanity and perspectives of incarcerated people often rendered invisible in mainstream discourse. These materials provide crucial resources for courses examining criminal justice, the history of incarceration, and the experiences of incarcerated populations, offering firsthand narratives that complement and complicate academic analyses of the carceral system.7

The HIV, AIDS & the Arts collection brings together artistic and activist materials documenting the cultural and social responses to the AIDS crisis, particularly within LGBTQ+ communities during the 1980s and 1990s.7,23 This collection includes artworks, publications, photographs, and other cultural artifacts that bear witness to both the devastating impact of the epidemic and the powerful creative and political responses it generated. By preserving these materials openly, Reveal Digital ensures that future generations can access crucial documentation of this transformative period in LGBTQ+ history and understand the central role of art and activism in responding to health crisis.

The Student Activism collection provides comprehensive documentation of student movements and activism throughout recent American history.7,24 This collection captures the deep and broad history of student organizing, protest, and engagement with social and political issues, offering primary source evidence of how young people have mobilized for change. These materials demonstrate the significant role student movements have played in broader social transformations, from civil rights activism through contemporary movements addressing climate change, racial justice, and educational equity.

The Behind the Scenes of the Civil Rights Movements collection represents one of Reveal Digital's most recent and ambitious initiatives, aiming to gather up to one million pages of primary source materials documenting civil rights activism across Black, Latina, Indigenous, Asian American, Pacific Islander, and Indigenous communities.1,10,1,25 Overseen by Jasmine Wilson, this collection deliberately centers lesser-known activists and organizations whose contributions to the civil rights movement have been historically marginalized in mainstream narratives.10,25 By amplifying these diverse voices, the collection expands understanding of civil rights movements beyond the most famous national leaders and organizations to encompass the grassroots activism that sustained and drove forward demands for justice and equality.

These collections collectively contain over seventy thousand digitized items and more than one million pages of openly available content.1,1 The diversity of collections demonstrates Reveal Digital's commitment to documenting multiple dimensions of twentieth-century social movements and marginalized communities. Rather than accepting traditional archival hierarchies that privilege certain voices and histories, Reveal Digital deliberately seeks out materials that fill critical gaps in the historical record, connecting underrepresented perspectives with global audiences and reshaping what history includes and who can access it.1,1

Technical Infrastructure and Digital Preservation Mechanisms

The technical foundations supporting Reveal Digital represent a sophisticated integration of digitization technologies, preservation standards, and discovery systems designed to ensure long-term accessibility and usability of digitized materials.1,12,1 Understanding these technical dimensions reveals how Reveal Digital achieves its commitment to permanent, barrier-free access.1,1

Long-term digital preservation through Portico provides the critical foundation ensuring that Reveal Digital collections will remain accessible across indefinite timescales despite technological obsolescence, format migration, and institutional changes.1,3,1,30 Portico functions as a certified digital repository specializing in preservation of scholarly content, offering institutional assurance that materials will persist and remain accessible even if hosting institutions cease operations or platforms change.1,1,32 This preservation commitment extends beyond merely storing digital files—it encompasses active preservation practices including format migration, metadata maintenance, and refreshing of storage media to guard against data degradation.12,30,32

The integration of long-term preservation capabilities through Portico directly addresses one of the most significant challenges in digital stewardship: ensuring that digitization investments produce materials that endure across generations rather than becoming inaccessible as technologies evolve and platforms disappear.12,30 Many early digitization projects created digital resources dependent on specific technologies or platforms that subsequently became obsolete, rendering materials effectively lost despite initial efforts to preserve them.12,30 By building preservation commitments into Reveal Digital's operational model from inception, the initiative ensures that crowdfunding investments produce genuinely permanent resources rather than temporary digital manifestations.

Hosting through JSTOR provides sophisticated discovery and access mechanisms that integrate Reveal Digital materials into existing research workflows while ensuring discoverability through multiple pathways.1,1 JSTOR's established search infrastructure, metadata integration capabilities, and global distribution network ensure that Reveal Digital materials reach scholarly audiences worldwide.1,1 The integration with JSTOR's broader discovery ecosystem means that researchers searching for materials on specific topics, time periods, or themes will encounter Reveal Digital collections alongside other scholarly resources, maximizing serendipitous discovery and citation.

The technical architecture supporting Reveal Digital also incorporates cloud-hosted platforms and AI-assisted processing tools emerging from JSTOR Digital Stewardship Services.12,30 These tools help institutions scale their digitization and collection processing work, addressing one of the most labor-intensive aspects of digital stewardship—preparing materials for discovery through comprehensive metadata creation and quality assurance.12,30 By leveraging artificial intelligence to assist with routine aspects of collection processing, institutions can focus human expertise on aspects requiring contextual knowledge, interpretive judgment, and understanding of specific communities and historical contexts.12,30

Metadata Standards and Descriptive Practices

The creation of consistent, high-quality metadata constitutes a critical component of Reveal Digital's operational infrastructure, enabling researchers to discover, understand, and effectively utilize digitized materials.1,1,34 Metadata—information about digital objects including titles, creators, dates, subjects, and relationships—serves as the crucial intermediary between raw digital materials and scholarly discovery.14,34,14

Reveal Digital implements metadata standards designed to ensure consistency and interoperability across its diverse collections while allowing flexibility to address unique characteristics of specific material types.1,1,34 Metadata standards function as formal guidelines providing consistency and interoperability across different systems and platforms.14,14 These standards act as a "data language," enabling systems and users to locate, understand, and leverage information efficiently by establishing consistent rules for tagging and organizing data details.14,14 In the context of Reveal Digital, metadata standards ensure that researchers searching across multiple collections or integrating Reveal Digital materials with other resources can reliably discover relevant materials and understand their relationships.

The selection of appropriate metadata standards involves balancing generality with specificity.34 Some metadata standards, such as Dublin Core, provide a relatively small, generic set of metadata elements that can be used by any community to describe and search across wide varieties of information resources.34 Other standards, including MARC (Machine-Readable Cataloging) standards traditionally used by libraries or EAD (Encoded Archival Description) standards developed for archival materials, offer more detailed, discipline-specific approaches to description.34,14 Reveal Digital's approach likely incorporates multiple complementary standards, using simpler schemas for broad discovery while allowing richer, more detailed metadata where collection-specific characteristics warrant such detail.34

The importance of metadata quality extends beyond mere organizational utility—well-crafted metadata fundamentally shapes how future researchers encounter and understand historical materials.14,34,14 Metadata that identifies and describes the creators, contexts, and relationships of primary sources provides crucial interpretive frameworks helping researchers understand not only what materials contain but also how to critically evaluate and situate them within broader historical contexts.34 This becomes particularly significant for Reveal Digital, where many materials document marginalized communities whose perspectives may differ substantially from dominant interpretations preserved in traditional archives.34

Automation represents an increasingly important dimension of metadata creation and maintenance processes.14,14 Given the volume of materials requiring description—Reveal Digital has digitized over one million pages—entirely manual metadata creation would require prohibitive labor investments.1,14,14 AI-assisted tools can accelerate routine aspects of metadata creation, such as optical character recognition for printed materials, language identification, and date extraction, allowing human expertise to focus on more interpretive and contextual aspects of description.12,14,14 Studies demonstrate that automation in metadata management can reduce required time investments by approximately fifty percent while improving consistency and accuracy.14,14

Rights Clearance and Copyright Management

Navigating copyright complexities represents one of the most significant challenges in digitizing historical materials, as determining who holds rights to publications, navigating permissions processes, and clearing rights for open access distribution requires substantial expertise and persistence.1,1,13 Reveal Digital's management of rights clearance constitutes one of its most distinctive contributions to the crowdfunding model, as individual institutions would struggle substantially with these complex processes independently.1,1,13

The process of rights clearance begins with identifying copyright holders and publishers for materials selected for inclusion in collections.37 For many historical materials spanning decades or centuries, determining rights holders can prove extraordinarily challenging.37 Publications may have changed hands multiple times, copyright status may be ambiguous, and some rights holders may no longer exist or be locatable.13,37 Despite these obstacles, Reveal Digital undertakes systematic approaches to identifying and contacting rights holders, seeking their permission for open access digitization and distribution.37

Digital rights management represents a broader technological and legal framework for managing how copyrighted digital content can be accessed, used, and distributed.43 While DRM technologies sometimes restrict legitimate research and educational use of copyrighted materials, the rights clearance processes that Reveal Digital undertakes serve legitimate purposes of respecting copyright holders' interests while enabling broader access.43,47 By securing explicit permissions for open access distribution, Reveal Digital ensures compliance with copyright law while simultaneously expanding access compared to arrangements where materials would remain privately held or behind subscription barriers.47

The rights clearance process creates reciprocal benefits for copyright holders and digitizing institutions.37 In exchange for granting permission for open access digitization, Reveal Digital provides copyright holders with high-resolution images of their publications, creating value for the copyright holder while incentivizing their cooperation with the digitization process.37 This approach acknowledges that many smaller publishers, activist organizations, and cultural institutions that produced materials documented in Reveal Digital collections may themselves benefit from high-resolution digital versions of their archival materials.37

The financial and labor investments required for comprehensive rights clearance justify the crowdfunding approach, as pooling resources across multiple institutions allows Reveal Digital to undertake the sustained, systematic work required to clear rights for large collections that individual institutions could not justify independently.1,1,13 When rights clearance forms part of the crowdfunding appeal to libraries, participating institutions understand that their funding directly enables this crucial work, making visible the previously invisible labor required to produce genuinely open access collections.1,1

Educational Impact and Integration with Open Educational Resources

Reveal Digital collections serve critical functions within educational contexts, providing primary source materials that enrich student learning and expose learners to diverse perspectives and historical narratives often marginalized in traditional textbooks.7,17,36 The integration of Reveal Digital materials into open educational resources represents a natural alignment between Reveal Digital's mission and broader movements toward accessible, affordable educational materials.7,17,36

Open educational resources represent teaching and learning materials available under open licenses permitting adaptation, modification, translation, and redistribution for educational purposes.17,36 By removing access barriers and permitting customization, open educational resources empower instructors to provide meaningful, contextualized learning materials addressing diverse student populations and learning objectives.17,36 Reveal Digital's provision of freely available primary sources creates essential building blocks for open educational resource creation, enabling educators to construct courses and learning materials incorporating primary source materials without requiring students to access expensive proprietary databases or content.7,17,36

The educational applications of Reveal Digital collections span diverse subject areas and disciplines. In criminal justice curricula, the American Prison Newspapers collection provides powerful primary source materials that bring new dimensions to students' understanding of incarceration systems, moving beyond abstract policy analysis to encounter firsthand narratives from incarcerated people themselves.7 In African American history courses, the Black Periodicals collection offers authentic voices and contemporary perspectives documenting diverse experiences and political perspectives within Black communities across historical periods.7,22 In courses examining LGBTQ+ history and activism, the HIV, AIDS & the Arts collection provides artistic and activist materials documenting cultural responses to the AIDS crisis.7,23

The pedagogical value of primary sources extends beyond simple information provision—primary sources engage students in authentic historical thinking, requiring them to interpret evidence, consider multiple perspectives, and develop critical awareness of how historical narratives are constructed.7,34 By providing access to materials documenting the perspectives of activists, artists, journalists, and community members, Reveal Digital enables educators to challenge master narratives and expand students' understanding of who constitutes legitimate historical actors.7,1 This represents particularly significant educational value for students whose own communities and perspectives have been historically marginalized in dominant educational narratives.1,36

The ability to seamlessly integrate Reveal Digital materials with open educational resources creates virtuous cycles of access and educational equity. Educators creating open educational resources can incorporate Reveal Digital materials without licensing restrictions or cost barriers, enabling the creation of genuinely open, comprehensive educational materials.7,17 Students accessing open educational resources constructed using Reveal Digital primary sources gain exposure to diverse perspectives while avoiding the textbook costs that represent substantial financial barriers, particularly for students from lower-income backgrounds experiencing digital access inequities.17,28,36

Challenges and Barriers in Collaborative Digital Preservation

While Reveal Digital's collaborative crowdfunding model represents an innovative approach to sustainable open access publishing, the initiative confronts substantial challenges related to building and maintaining collaborative relationships, managing technical complexity, and addressing persistent inequities in digital access and digital literacy.26,27,28

Collaboration between research libraries and community archives—organizations often holding unique primary source materials documenting marginalized communities—presents particular complexities requiring sustained attention to relationship building and power dynamics.26 Research institutions often approach community partnerships with paternalistic attitudes, viewing community archives as repositories requiring "rescue" through digitization and preservation by professional institutions.26 Such approaches, though well-intentioned, can undermine community agency and control over their own cultural heritage, replicating historical patterns where outsiders extract community resources without meaningful consultation or benefit.26

Addressing these collaborative challenges requires research libraries and digitization initiatives to reconstruct relationships around principles of mutual respect, genuine reciprocity, and community control.26 This means actively listening to community partners regarding their priorities, aspirations, and concerns about how their materials are described, preserved, and made accessible.26 It means developing flexible membership structures and participation models allowing organizations with limited resources to participate as partners rather than merely as content providers.26 And it requires explicitly addressing technical and labor barriers that might prevent smaller organizations from meeting institutional requirements for participation.26

Digital access inequities represent another significant barrier limiting the impact of Reveal Digital's mission to expand access to knowledge.28 Despite global internet expansion, substantial portions of populations remain offline or possess only limited digital connectivity.19,28 Within populations with internet access, significant disparities persist in the quality and reliability of connections, with lower-income households and people of color disproportionately relying on smartphones and cellular connections rather than broadband home internet access.28 These inequities mean that Reveal Digital's freely accessible online collections remain unavailable to populations experiencing digital access barriers.28

Beyond device and connectivity access, digital literacy—the skills required to effectively navigate, search, and utilize digital resources—varies significantly across populations.28 Many adult learners in the United States lack sufficient digital skills to independently locate and utilize online resources, with digital skill gaps particularly pronounced among older adults and individuals with limited educational attainment.28 These barriers suggest that open access to digital collections, while necessary, proves insufficient without accompanying efforts to build digital literacy and ensure that populations most likely to benefit from historical materials documenting their communities possess the skills and infrastructure required to access them.28

The sustainability of the crowdfunding model itself, while innovative, requires careful attention to evolving institutional commitments and technological changes.1,27 Libraries making crowdfunding commitments to Reveal Digital do so from existing budgets that face ongoing constraints and competing priorities.27 The model depends on stable institutional commitment to funding digitization projects, which may prove challenging if broader economic pressures or shifting institutional priorities redirect library resources to other purposes.27 Additionally, maintaining long-term preservation and access through Portico requires ongoing institutional commitments and operational funding, meaning that Reveal Digital's collections depend on the continued viability of both Portico and JSTOR as preservation and hosting platforms.1,12,30

Collaboration with Research Libraries and Archives

The success of Reveal Digital depends fundamentally on partnerships with archives, libraries, and cultural organizations willing to commit both funding and contribution of primary source materials to specific projects.1,3,1,44 These partnerships involve complex negotiations regarding which materials warrant inclusion, how materials will be described and presented, what metadata will accompany digitized items, and how intellectual property and attribution will be handled.1,1,26

Libraries and cultural institutions contributing materials to Reveal Digital projects maintain significant agency in defining collection scope and priorities.1,3,1,37 In some instances, participating libraries can influence what materials get prioritized for inclusion—for example, participating UK libraries contributing to the Independent Voices collection can set content priorities regarding which UK alternative press materials should be included.37 This collaborative approach ensures that collections reflect priorities and perspectives of diverse institutional communities rather than concentrating power in centralized decision-making.37

The collaborative relationship extends beyond collection definition to encompass shared responsibility for the digitization workflow.1,1 While Reveal Digital manages rights clearance, digitization quality assurance, and metadata creation centrally, contributing institutions provide raw materials and expertise regarding their specific holdings and contexts.1,1 This division of labor leverages comparative advantages—Reveal Digital develops specialized expertise in rights clearance and large-scale digitization management while institutions contribute contextual knowledge regarding materials and communities.1,1

Furthermore, participating libraries receive back digital files of the materials they contribute, enabling them to incorporate digitized materials into their own digital collections and research infrastructure.37 This reciprocal benefit model recognizes that institutions making financial contributions and contributing materials deserve value beyond merely contributing to a shared public resource.37 By providing digital copies to contributing institutions, Reveal Digital ensures that crowdfunding results in enhanced institutional collections, not merely in public-spirited donations to distant initiatives.37

Metadata Standards and Technical Interoperability

The technical standards and practices underlying Reveal Digital's collections reflect broader developments in digital information management regarding how to structure descriptive information enabling discovery, access, and interoperability across diverse systems and platforms.14,34,14 Understanding these technical dimensions reveals how Reveal Digital enables researchers and educators to discover and utilize its materials effectively.14,34,14

Metadata standards address fundamental challenges posed by exponential growth in digital information.14,14 With over 328 million terabytes of new data created daily, the challenge extends far beyond storing information to encompassing how to organize, describe, and retrieve information efficiently.14,14 Metadata standards establish consistent rules for tagging and organizing data details, allowing seamless data interoperability and enabling systems to interpret and utilize information effectively.14,14 In the context of Reveal Digital, consistent metadata enables researchers to search across multiple collections, discover related materials, and understand relationships between items and collections.14,34,14

The Getty Museum's Introduction to Metadata provides useful frameworks for categorizing metadata standards into content standards, technical standards, and interoperability standards.34 Content standards govern what elements should be described and what values those elements should contain—for example, standards specifying how to represent dates, creator names, or subject classifications.34 Technical standards establish file formats and syntax rules ensuring data storage compatibility—examples include XML (Extensible Markup Language) and JSON (JavaScript Object Notation), markup languages enabling structured data representation across diverse systems.14,34,14 Interoperability standards, including Dublin Core, EAD, MARC, and other schemas, establish common data elements enabling different systems to exchange and understand metadata created according to different specialized standards.34,14

Reveal Digital's metadata practices likely employ multiple complementary standards addressing different aspects of description and discovery.34 Dublin Core provides broad, generic metadata elements enabling simple resource description and cross-system searching.34 More detailed descriptive standards may apply to specific collection types—archival materials might utilize EAD standards capturing hierarchical relationships between collections, series, and items.34 By implementing multiple compatible standards, Reveal Digital balances the need for consistent, discoverable metadata against the need to capture collection-specific information reflecting unique characteristics of diverse materials.34

Integration with JSTOR Digital Stewardship Services

The recent emergence of JSTOR Digital Stewardship Services represents an important evolution in the ITHAKA ecosystem supporting digital collection management and preservation, creating complementary capabilities to Reveal Digital's crowdfunded collections approach.12,30 Understanding this relationship illuminates how ITHAKA's various initiatives work synergistically to advance digital preservation and open access.12,30

JSTOR Digital Stewardship Services provides a seamless, cloud-hosted platform helping institutions process, manage, preserve, and share collections while expanding global access.12,30 The platform integrates digital asset management capabilities, long-term preservation through Portico, and AI-assisted collections processing tools within unified infrastructure.12,30 Rather than requiring institutions to assemble separate, incompatible systems for different aspects of collection management—separate platforms for storage, preservation, discovery, and access—Digital Stewardship Services provides integrated functionality addressing the full lifecycle of collection management.12,30

AI-assisted processing tools represent a particularly significant innovation in Digital Stewardship Services, addressing labor-intensive bottlenecks that have historically limited institutions' digitization capacity.12,30 Optical character recognition for printed materials, metadata extraction, language identification, and format validation represent routine tasks consuming substantial human labor while not requiring interpretive judgment or contextual expertise.12,30 By automating these routine processes, Digital Stewardship Services frees institutional staff to focus on more meaningful, interpretive aspects of stewardship requiring contextual knowledge and professional judgment.12,30

While Reveal Digital and Digital Stewardship Services serve somewhat different niches, they complement each other within ITHAKA's broader ecosystem.12,1,30 Reveal Digital's crowdfunded, collaborative model addresses materials and collections insufficient to attract individual institutional investment but collectively important to the scholarly record.1,1 Digital Stewardship Services supports institutions managing their own distinctive collections, providing technical infrastructure and tools that would exceed the capacity of many institutions to develop independently.12,30 Together, these initiatives demonstrate ITHAKA's commitment to supporting digital preservation and access across diverse pathways and institutional contexts.12,30

Future Directions and Emerging Opportunities

Looking forward, Reveal Digital faces both opportunities and challenges that will shape the initiative's evolution and impact over coming years. The ongoing expansion of collection scope, integration with educational resources, and deepening partnerships with communities and institutions all represent significant opportunities for extending Reveal Digital's impact.1,7,10,1,16

The Behind the Scenes of the Civil Rights Movements collection represents an ambitious future direction, with plans to gather up to one million pages documenting civil rights activism across diverse communities.1,10,1,25 This collection's explicit focus on lesser-known activists and organizations demonstrates Reveal Digital's commitment to continuously expanding the historical record beyond what traditional archives have prioritized.10,25 As this collection progresses toward completion and publication, it will provide scholars and educators with unprecedented access to materials documenting the grassroots activism driving civil rights movements.10,25

The integration of Reveal Digital collections with emerging technologies and pedagogical approaches presents additional opportunities. As discussed previously, the alignment between Reveal Digital's freely available primary sources and the open educational resources movement creates potential for deepening partnerships with educators and educational technology platforms.7,17,36 Generative AI and other emerging technologies may create new possibilities for discovering connections across materials, generating research questions, and making historical materials more accessible to diverse audiences.17 However, such developments also raise important questions regarding copyright, intellectual property, and appropriate use of primary sources that Reveal Digital and the broader scholarly community will need to navigate carefully.17

Addressing persistent digital access and digital literacy inequities represents a critical challenge for ensuring that Reveal Digital's freely available collections genuinely serve marginalized communities whose histories they document.28,35,36 Simply making materials available online proves insufficient if populations lack connectivity, devices, or digital skills required to access them.28 This suggests potential future directions involving partnerships with public libraries, community organizations, and educational institutions in underserved communities to provide supported access to Reveal Digital materials alongside digital literacy programming.28,36

The sustainability of crowdfunding as a model for long-term collection development and preservation also warrants continued attention and potentially innovative refinements.1,27 As the number of collections grows and libraries establish regular commitments to Reveal Digital projects, the model may need to evolve to address changing institutional circumstances and emerging categories of materials warranting digitization.1,27 Strengthening institutional commitment mechanisms, diversifying funding sources, and ensuring equitable participation from institutions of all sizes will be important to the model's long-term health.1,27

Conclusion: Reveal Digital as a Model for Equitable Digital Stewardship

Reveal Digital exemplifies how innovative organizational models, collaborative relationships, and sustained commitment to social justice can transform how societies preserve and provide access to historical materials.1,3,1,16 Rather than accepting the traditional archival hierarchies that privilege certain voices and institutions while marginalizing others, Reveal Digital deliberately seeks out and preserves materials documenting the experiences of activists, artists, journalists, and community members whose perspectives have historically been excluded from mainstream archives.1,10,1

The crowdfunding model that underpins Reveal Digital demonstrates that sustainable, equitable digital preservation is possible when institutions commit to collaborative approaches redistributing agency and decision-making authority among diverse stakeholders.1,3,1 By enabling libraries of all sizes to collectively fund digitization projects, Reveal Digital has shown that the historical record need not be determined solely by the wealthiest research institutions or largest publishers. Instead, library communities can collectively shape what gets preserved and made accessible for future generations.1,3,1

Over one million pages of openly available content across seven major collections already demonstrate Reveal Digital's significant impact on scholarly resources.1,1 These collections provide invaluable materials for researchers, educators, and students seeking to understand twentieth-century social movements, engage with diverse historical perspectives, and challenge dominant narratives that marginalize communities' experiences and contributions.7,10,1,22,23,24,25 The materials documented in Reveal Digital collections belong in classrooms, libraries, and research communities worldwide, and Reveal Digital's commitment to open access ensures they can reach these constituencies without cost barriers.1,7,1

The technical infrastructure supporting Reveal Digital—including long-term preservation through Portico, sophisticated metadata standards, rigorous rights clearance processes, and integration with JSTOR's discovery ecosystem—ensures that digitization investments produce genuinely durable, discoverable, accessible resources.1,12,1,30,34 These technical commitments reflect recognition that open access alone proves insufficient without accompanying commitments to permanent preservation, quality description, and global discoverability.1,12,1

Looking forward, Reveal Digital's continued evolution will benefit from sustained attention to the complex challenges of collaborative relationship-building, addressing persistent digital inequities, and ensuring that materials genuinely serve the communities whose histories they document.26,28,16 By maintaining focus on social justice motivations, deepening partnerships with marginalized communities, and developing innovative solutions to barriers limiting equitable access, Reveal Digital can extend its impact while remaining grounded in the commitment to changing who gets to be part of history.1,1,16
